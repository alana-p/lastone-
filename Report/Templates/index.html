Sleep

Many students sacrifice their sleep without even realizing the effects it has on students and their overall performance in school.

Why is sleep important?

  • Good sleep can decrease mood swings
  • Increase productivity
  • Increase mental health
  • Is essential for a good memory

A good rest is important as it allows the brain to reset.

Sleep Deprivation

Sleep deprivation is very serious and affects many young people.

Sleep deprivation is "the situation or condition of suffering from a lack of sleep" according to the Oxford Dictionary and can severely affect the mental health of students and how they perform in school.

The symptoms of Sleep deprivation are as follows:

  • Daytime drowsiness
  • Unable to concentrate
  • Memory problems
  • Poor school performance
  • Problems with impulse control

Resources of Students

While sleep does affect a student's overall performance, there are other overlooked factors, such as the resources available to a student.

Poverty affects students early on in their learning. There is an evident gap between well-off people and those who aren't. Especially in school, as studies have proven in Ireland, students from impoverished families test the lowest in maths and reading.

This is simply because poorer students are less likely to:

  • Do grinds classes as they cost money
  • Go to the library
  • Spend time studying after school as their families are usually very dependent on them
  • Be attentive in class due to hunger or worrying about family members

Attendance in school

Students often disregard school attendance, but it hugely impacts how well students do in school.

According to 'The Irish Examiner': Children in second class in 2023, who would have experienced COVID school closures while in junior infants, demonstrated a higher level of worry and anxiety compared to those who attended second class in 2019.

This clearly shows us that not being in school for a long time can affect how students feel about school and studying.

Other effects of not attending school are:

  • Social Isolation
  • Falling behind in grades
  • Stress and anxiety
